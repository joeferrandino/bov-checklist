Property Information Checklist: Broker Opinion of Value (BOV)

Choose the level of analysis that fits your timeline and goals.
Option A
Comprehensive BOV
A thorough valuation analysis derived from actual financial records.
Information to Submit
  • Current rent roll reflecting all occupied and vacant spaces
  • Full copies of leases (or detailed lease abstracts)
  • Trailing 12-month operating expense statement (P&L)
  • Any pending lease negotiations, renewals, or known tenant issues
  • Major capital improvements completed in the last 5 years (roof, HVAC, paving, etc.)
The Benefits
  • Provides the clarity needed to make informed decisions about the asset's future
  • Uncovers rollover risk, expense leakage, and untapped value opportunity
  • Establishes actual NOI based on verified income and expense data
  • Underwriting that holds up to buyer scrutiny
Timeline Typically 5–9 business days, depending on full submission of documents.
Option B
Preliminary BOV
A high-level valuation analysis based on general property information.
Information to Submit
  • Occupancy summary: tenant name or suite number, square footage, monthly rent, lease start and end dates, any month-to-month tenancies, and vacant spaces
  • Annual operating expenses: real estate taxes, insurance, electric, heating fuel, water & sewer, trash removal, repairs & maintenance, landscaping, and snow removal
  • Other recurring costs: property management, payroll, contract services, and CAM reconciliations
  • Tenant vs. owner responsibility: note which utilities and expenses are tenant-paid, owner-paid, or shared
  • Major capital improvements completed in the last 5 years (roof, HVAC, paving, etc.)
The Benefits
  • A credible valuation estimate supported by market data
  • Helpful for internal planning or initial conversations
  • No formal financials or lease documents needed to get started
Timeline Typically 2–4 business days.
